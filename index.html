What is this?

Good, truthful scenework requires attaining a state of freefall — allowing yourself to find peace in not knowing what will come next. Like really not knowing. Entangl is an attempt to take away the last peice of an improviser's safety net: the comfort that someone will edit or modify a scene for them.

Entangl is primarily a tool for coaches to drill scenework in the rehearsal room. Set how long a given scene might last, and you'll get a random duration within that range. Two people go out and try to do a truthful, relationship-driven scene. The scene doesn't end until the timer hits zero. No sweeps, no walk-ons, no tag-outs, no kooky group games or flashback wiggle fingers. A horn goes off when time is up, a new random time is generated, and it starts over again.

Why is this useful? If a scene might last for a whole 3 minutes, performers won't be able to rely on jokes or gimmicks, which lose steam quickly. On the other hand, a scene as short as 30 seconds requires them to find their characters, environment, and relationship relatively quickly.

With the knowledge that no one is going to stop or change a scene for them, and no clue when it will end, the only thing left for them to do is improv.
